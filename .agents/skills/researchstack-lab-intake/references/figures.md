# Figure and Table Guidance

Use figures to answer a question, not merely to display data.

For every figure or table:

- State the reader question first.
- Use axis labels with units.
- Mark whether higher or lower is better.
- Keep legends near the data when possible.
- Make captions self-contained: setup, workload, metric, and take-away.
- Call out variance, confidence intervals, or repeated-trial summary when relevant.
- Avoid cherry-picked scales that exaggerate tiny improvements.

Preferred figure types by use:

- Mechanism overview: clean pipeline or architecture diagram.
- Main result: grouped bars or line plots with direct baseline comparison.
- Sensitivity or scaling: line plots with clear x-axis semantics.
- Ablation: compact bar/table with one factor changed at a time.
- Error analysis: categorized bars or confusion-style breakdown.

Use tables when exact numbers matter more than trend shape.
