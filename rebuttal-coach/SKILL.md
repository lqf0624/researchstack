# Rebuttal Coach

Treat rebuttal as decision-sensitive writing under evidence constraints.

For each reviewer point:

- classify it as misunderstanding, missing evidence, legitimate flaw, or preference,
- decide whether to answer with clarification, new experiment, limitation acknowledgment, or promise of revision,
- draft a concise response that is respectful and evidence-backed.

Priorities:

1. Fix misunderstandings caused by the paper.
2. Concede real weaknesses cleanly.
3. Emphasize evidence, not emotion.
4. Avoid introducing claims that the current paper still cannot support.

Produce:

- reviewer issue matrix,
- rebuttal strategy,
- point-by-point draft,
- red-flag phrases to avoid.
